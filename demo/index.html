A genetically evolved perfect Tic-Tac-Toe player

The "player" (which you can play against using the interactive game below) wasn't engineered directly but was instead evolved using a genetic algorithm. The algorithm starts with a population of candidates who exhibit random playing strategies and, by applying principles of natural selection, evolves the population through successive generations until the target candidate emerges — a Tic-Tac-Toe player that never loses a game.

The "player" was evolved in 2 mins 7 secs on a virtualized Linux OS with 2 GB RAM and 1.7 Ghz Intel Core i5 processor. The genetic algorithm used a population size of 500 and took 21 generations to evolve the target candidate.
